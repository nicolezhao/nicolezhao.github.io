Design Project: Low Fidelity Prototypes

Three low fidelity prototypes that tackle the problem of elderly adults slipping and falling on the stairs.

1. A traction device that uses friction between the strips under the user's shoes and the traction pads on the stairs to prevent slipping. The two materials work in tandem and is lightweight and does not interfere with the user's activities at home. This design concept was created through personas, mind-mapping, surveys, and a QFD. The prototype has moved on to the medium fidelety prototyping phase.

2. A standing stair lift that minimizes the tension in the user's knees from sitting down on a conventional stair lift. Smooth rollers are used to ensure a safe ride. A standing stair lift is beneficial because it reduces time, space and effort compared to a sitting stair lift.
